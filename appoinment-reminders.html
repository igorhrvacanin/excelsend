Appointment Reminders

We’ll be covering two different appointment reminders on this page. The first will show you how to set appointment reminders days in advance, and the second will show you how to set appointment reminders hours in advance.

Appointment Reminder - Days

Our example spreadsheet contains appointment information for our clients.

The Text Message

We can merge information from this spreadsheet into an appointment reminder message for each customer.

Hi ^^name^^, a reminder of your appointment on ^^date{d:dddd}^^ ^^date{d:ddx}^^ ^^date{d:MMMM}^^ at ^^date{d:HH:mm}^^. Please reply to confirm attendance

With these merge fields we are extracting the name of the day, the day of the month, the month name, and the time all from the date field in the spreadsheet. The preview for the first message looks like this:

Hi Brian, a reminder of your appointment on 5th June at 10:30. Please reply to confirm attendance

Finally, we click the ‘Send Messages’ button to submit the message Job and each personalized message is sent through the ExcelSend service to each individual’s mobile device.

Appointment Reminder - Days

You can instruct ExcelSend (Desktop Version Only) on when to send each individual text message in a single Job. You do this by telling ExcelSend to use the appointment time as the time to send the message with an adjustment of course, so they get it in advance of the appointment. Our sample spreadsheet contains some appointment information for our three clients. They have appointments at 10:00, 11:00, and 12:00. We open the spreadsheet in ExcelSend Desktop, type in or load our message, and in the ‘Schedule’ section we pick ‘Send Later’ and ‘Date/Time’ from Sheet. Next we tell ExcelSend which column contains the date. Finally, in the ‘Options’ section type in the number of minutes before each appointment time you want the message to go. Click the ‘Review’ button and see how the send times differ for each message.
